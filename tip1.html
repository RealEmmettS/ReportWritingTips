Tip #1

Organization

Organize your reports chronologically, and make it clear whether your report goes forward chronologically (from the beginning of the incident to the end), or backwards chronologically (from the end of the incident to the beginning). Both ways are usually acceptable, as long as you specify which one you chose and why you went forward (Ex: This report starts at the beginning to make it clear how the events transpired) or backward (Ex: This report is written in the order of events the victim described to me). To make this even easier for someone else to read and understand, consider grouping time periods together in their own paragraphs. 

It is very important that your report is clear and easily-understandable. There should be little room for the prosecutor or defense to misinterpret information that came from your reports.
