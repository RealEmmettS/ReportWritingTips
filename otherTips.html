Other Tips

1. Try limiting yourself to one idea per sentence. Continuing on with an idea through a few sentences is ok, just don't cram three different ideas into one sentence

2. Start every sentence with a person, place, or thing. Make it obvious what your talking about.

3. Be clear and specific. Don't say "the man traveled", say "John traveled west on I-423 in his personal sedan".

4. Simplify your speech. Saying "about" will save you time and make it easier to read than if you had said "pertaining to...".

5. Don't worry about grammar. According to the FBI, most officers tend to focus on grammar and perfect well-rounded writing. Try to turn your attention more to the facts and correct diction styles. Grammar is easier to fix than misinformation.

6. Don't rush. Although the report you're writing may be due the next day, don't rush it. Relax yourself and your mind, and write at your own pace. Of course, don't be writing your report one minute before the deadline, but take you time and take care in what you're writing.

7. Avoid jargon. Most of the time, prosecutors and jurors alike will hear portions of your reports, so unless it's necessary to convey your point, keep the jargon out of it.

8. Don't write a novel. Unless your name is J.K. Rowling, Mark Twain, or Shakespeare, don't try and write the next best seller. Paint a picture and make details specific, but don't overdo it.

9. Ask for help. If you have time, have a colleague, friend, or family member proofread your report to make sure it all makes sense and flows nicely. Although, don't do this if the investigation is supposed to be confidential or not open to the public :)
