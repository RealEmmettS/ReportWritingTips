Tip #2

Set The Scene

When writing a report after a few long days and nights, it's easy to forget the little things. Don't forget to set the scene to make it clear exactly what happened (remember every small detail counts). After your opening statement, state the address (if applicable), time of day (24h and 12h), place name (if applicable), direction (facing the counter/looking away from the storefront), and rough number of other people around the incident(if applicable). Do this anytime the scene changes, although if the next scene is in the same area (Ex: in the back of the store instead of the front of the store where the incident began), the address and place name may be omitted.


 Not only does this gives all parties thorough insight into exactly when and where the crime happened (which can make or break a case), but it also looks good on you as an officer. This 'attention to detail' will help you out when you're being considered for a promotion or other similar position.
